While overall pipeline throughput remains mathematically high (250.3%) due to burst discharges, a low baseline daily discharge rate (2.38%) compared to the massive in-care population (~12,000) routinely triggers custody backlogs at the CBP level.

## 2. KEY OPERATIONAL BOTTLENECKS

* **Capacity Imbalance:** The system manages a static load of approximately 12,000 children, but daily operations move only 200-400 children. The system operates at near-maximum holding capacity, meaning border spikes immediately create CBP backlogs.
* **Sponsor Vetting Friction:** The 2.38% Discharge Effectiveness confirms that the primary constraint is the rigorous, time-consuming nature of the sponsor vetting and approval process.

## 3. PRIORITY ACTION ITEMS

1. **Expedite the Placement Phase:** Dedicate supplementary administrative resources to the sponsor vetting process. Utilizing automated pre-screening tools can increase the daily discharge rate, freeing up HHS bed space.
2. **Establish Surge Buffers:** The observed Transfer Efficiency (69.1%) indicates CBP facilities are holding children while waiting for HHS placement. Deploying temporary surge-capacity facilities can help align efficiency with dynamic target thresholds.
3. **Adopt Live Threshold Monitoring:** Institutionalize the use of the Streamlit Live Analytics Dashboard to monitor the Backlog Accumulation Rate and Outcome Stability Score in real-time, enabling preemptive logistical adjustments.
